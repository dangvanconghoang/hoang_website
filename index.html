About Me

I am an independent researcher interested in causal inference and its foundations. My research focuses on robustness and falsification: understanding when treatment-effect claims should be trusted, how localized violations of identifying assumptions can be detected, and how causal decisions can be made reliably under specification uncertainty.

More broadly, I am interested in semiparametric methods, uncertainty-aware decision-making, and trustworthy causal inference.

Research Interests
  • Robust Causal Inference
  • Causal Falsification & Sensitivity Analysis
  • Decision-Making Under Uncertainty
  • Semiparametric Theory
